Thai Coconut Curry Beef with Vegetables

Per serving: ~530 cal, 48g protein, 16g carbs, 30g fat. Week 1 rotation lunch.

Servings: 5 Prep: 15 minutes Cook: 30 minutes
Tip: Click the AnyList extension icon in your Edge toolbar to import this recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2.25 lbs lean ground beef (90/10)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 lb broccoli florets
  • 2 red bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 lb green beans, trimmed and halved
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges for serving

Directions

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium-high. Brown ground beef, breaking it up, about 8 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and ginger. Cook 3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in curry paste and cook 1 minute. Pour in coconut milk and fish sauce. Simmer 5 minutes.
  4. Add broccoli, bell peppers, and green beans. Cover and cook 8-10 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Stir in lime juice. Cool, then portion into 5 containers (~1.75 cups each).
  6. Top with cilantro after reheating.

Notes: Freezes and reheats beautifully. Add cilantro after reheating, not before.
