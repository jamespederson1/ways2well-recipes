4 Thu 4: Lamb & Mushroom Stew over Cauliflower Rice

Per serving: ~530 cal, 44g protein, 16g carbs, 32g fat. Thursday — Strength & Minerals theme. Lamb is the highest-zinc red meat; mushrooms add magnesium and ergothioneine.

Servings: 4 Prep: 15 minutes Cook: 30 minutes
Tip: Click the AnyList extension icon in your Edge toolbar to import this recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1.8 lbs ground lamb (or 50/50 lamb-beef blend)
  • 1.5 lbs cremini mushrooms, quartered
  • 1.5 lbs frozen cauliflower rice
  • 2 carrots, small dice
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp arrowroot starch (or cornstarch)
  • 2 tsp dried rosemary
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in Dutch oven or large skillet. Brown lamb 6 minutes, breaking apart. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add onion, carrots, and garlic, cook 5 minutes. Stir in tomato paste, Worcestershire, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper, 1 minute.
  3. Add mushrooms and 1.5 cups of the broth. Simmer covered 15 minutes until mushrooms release liquid and carrots are tender.
  4. Whisk arrowroot into remaining 1/2 cup cold broth. Stir into stew, simmer uncovered 2-3 minutes until lightly thickened.
  5. Cook cauliflower rice in dry skillet 6-8 minutes until dry. Toss with butter and salt.
  6. Layer cauliflower rice in 4 containers, top with stew. Cool completely, freeze flat.

Notes: Replaces the original Lamb Shepherd's Pie (which required oven reheat from frozen — the cauliflower mash top watered out badly in microwave). Same Strength & Minerals profile (lamb zinc/iron/B12, mushroom magnesium, beef broth collagen), now in a stew-over-rice format that microwaves cleanly. Arrowroot keeps the gravy from breaking on reheat.
